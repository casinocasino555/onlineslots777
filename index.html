Freebie in games: past, present, future

Everyone loves free. Sometimes this fact is explained by Maslow's theory: the desire to get more for less comes from a subconscious desire to save energy. The theory, however, has already been recognized as questionable, but the love for freebies remains undeniable.

You could always get something for free in games. At first, no one considered them as a way to generate income at all. But even when they started to make money on games, there were many opportunities left for lovers of free buns.

For the same Doom, the company distributed the sources of network drivers and a level editor for free. Thanks to the well thought out and understandable organization of the editor, almost anyone could create new levels and share them with other users. Not exactly a freebie, but a nice opportunity to modify your favorite game for yourself.

Attention to new products

Sometimes it was possible not even to buy the game, but to wait for its free distribution analogue, which could often support and edit levels from other similar games. For example, Boulder Dash had quite a few analogues, which was so popular at one time that it was adapted for several platforms of slot machines (the reverse process happened often, but the chosen ones got to the machine from the PC).

When the market grew, instead of a few games a year, competing companies started releasing several hundred. Consumers found it increasingly difficult to choose something from a huge number of names, and manufacturers - to draw attention to new products. As a result, shareware appeared. At first, this term was used to denote software for which the author asked to pay, although he distributed it for free. Later, this became the name for techniques that encouraged users to fork out. The most popular of them was freemium (free + premium) - the game was distributed freely, but it was either limited in functionality compared to the full version, or stopped launching after the trial period expired. In any case, the player could evaluate the game before buying, and the manufacturer could collect opinions and finalize the product.
